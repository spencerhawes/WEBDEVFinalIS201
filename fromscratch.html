Best Practices

Out on the course it is easy to get caught up in the moment. The following list will help you to pick the best shot for each situation you face.

  1. Assess Your Lie
    • Are you in the fairway, rough, or a hazard? What kind of contact can you make with the ball?
    • Is your lie uphill, downhill, etc.... How will the slope affect your shot
    • What is between you and where you want to go? Where is trouble?

  2. How are the Conditions
    • What is the weather doing? Where is the wind? If raining, how will it affect your shot?
    • How is the course playing? Slow or Fast? Are shots sticking or rolling a lot?

  3. What is your Ability
    • What is your natural shot? How will it play?
    • What is your most common miss? Where will it end up?
    • Take enough club for the distance you need. Be conservative and club if necessary.

  4. Execute the Shot
    • Pick a target that will minimize the damage of a miss
    • Commit to a shot! Pick what you will do and picture it in your mind.
    • Step up and hit a good one!
